## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

Help our residents with UI to be as safe and independent as possible with utilization of strategies, exercises, and modalities

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### PT/OT:

- Patient will manage incontinence products independently 5/5 sessions.
- Patient will demonstrate toilet transfers from WC using grab bar with modified independence using grab bar.
- Patient will stand for two minutes to complete clothing management before and after toileting with SBA 5/5 sessions.
- Patient will demonstrate ability to complete hygiene after toileting with minimal assist for 5/5 sessions to decrease risk for compromised skin integrity.
- Patient will perform sit to stand transfers without episodes of UI for 5/5 sessions.
- Patient will decrease nighttime trips to toilet from six to four to improve safety.
- Patient will decrease episodes of UI from 5 to 3 per day for 5/5 days.
- Patient will not have UI with simulated coughing/sneezing 5/5 sessions.
- Patient will decrease bladder irritant intake from four to two servings per day.
- Patient will increase water intake from 20 ounces to 60 ounces per day.
- Patient will demonstrate belly breath and initial upright transverse activation with 50% tactile cues to improve sitting tolerance.
- Patient will report 4/10 pain after sitting with in WC with back support for 30 minutes.
- Pt will demonstrate increased standing tolerance with less pain and improved balance after 10 min of active sitting with feet on the vibration plate.
- Pt will be able to exhale and engage core while independently changing positions in bed and moving from supine to sit with SBA reporting low back pain <3/10.
- Patient's severity level rating of urinary incontinence will decrease to 8/10 to increase participation in functional activities and decrease fear of going places.
- Patient will decrease the number of incontinence episodes at night to 2 in order to improve sleep patterns, facilitate safe functional mobility and decrease risk for falls.
- Patient will exhibit a decrease in the number of incontinent urge episodes per day to 9 to decrease fear related to incontinence, facilitate safe functional mobility, and improve functional task performance.
