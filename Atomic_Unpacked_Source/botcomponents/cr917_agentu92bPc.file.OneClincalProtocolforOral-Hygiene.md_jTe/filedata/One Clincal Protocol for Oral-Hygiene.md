## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

### SLP:

- Maintain adequate oral hygiene to reduce oral bacterial load
- Prevent dental diseases and oral infections
- Reduce risk of aspiration pneumonia by decreasing bacteria that can be aspirated
- Prevent infection and reduce aspiration pneumonia risk
- Keep the mouth comfortable for safe eating and drinking
- Manage oral pain or dryness that may limit intake
- Support safe and adequate nutrition and hydration
- Develop or restore ability to perform oral care tasks as part of ADLs
- Sequence steps, use adaptive equipment, or improve problem-solving for self-hygiene
- Improve independence and participation in daily self-care
- Prevention of Aspiration Pneumonia by providing thorough oral hygiene
- Maintain or improve oral function needed for speaking, swallowing, and social interaction
- Address swallowing safety and functional communication
- Assess safest and most effective oral care techniques
- Optimize positioning to perform oral hygiene safely
- Train caregivers on individualized methods and schedules
- Determine and train best methods and positioning
- Prevent discomfort, halitosis, and embarrassment
- Support resident preferences and routines whenever possible
- Respect dignity and quality of life

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### SLP Goals:

- Patient will participate in an oral care routine (e.g., brushing teeth, tongue, gums, rinsing mouth) to reduce oral bacterial load prior to meals in 80% of observed opportunities.
- Patient will safely swish and spit mouthwash with minimal loss of bolus control to improve safety during oral care on 4/5 opportunities.
- Patient will allow use of suction during oral hygiene routine with caregivers on 90% of attempts to decrease risk of aspiration during oral care.
- Patient will perform thorough oral hygiene (including brushing teeth, tongue, gums, and rinsing) prior to each water intake per Free Water Protocol in 90% of opportunities to reduce bacterial load and decrease risk of aspiration pneumonia.
- Patient will sequence written steps for brushing teeth with 90% accuracy to improve follow-through with oral hygiene tasks.
- Patient will demonstrate recall of oral hygiene tasks with use of a memory aid (e.g., checklist, posted sign) with minimal cues in 80% of opportunities.

### OT Goals:

- Patient will perform oral hygiene tasks (e.g., brushing teeth, rinsing, denture care) with setup assistance and adaptive equipment as needed to improve independence in self-care in 80% of opportunities.
- Patient will retrieve, organize, and return oral care items from a bathroom or bedside storage area with minimal cues to increase independence and safety in oral hygiene tasks.
- Patient will demonstrate safe standing balance at the sink for up to 5 minutes with contact guard assist while completing oral hygiene to reduce fall risk during ADL performance.
- Patient will sequence all steps of an oral care routine with the use of a visual checklist with no more than minimal verbal cues in 4/5 sessions.
- Patient will demonstrate use of adaptive oral care tools (e.g., built-up handle toothbrush, long-handled mirror) with minimal verbal cues to compensate for decreased grip strength and reach in 80% of observed opportunities.
- Patient will comply with daily oral hygiene routine as part of morning self-care ADL session with supervision to maximize carryover and reduce caregiver burden.

### PT Goals:

- Patient will maintain dynamic sitting balance at the sink for 5 minutes while performing oral hygiene with supervision to reduce fall risk.
- Patient will tolerate standing for 3 minutes at the sink with modified independence to complete oral hygiene.

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### SLP:

- Oral Health Assessment Tool (OHAT)
- Risk assessment to determine Aspiration risk including:
  - Three Pillars of Aspiration
  - BOLUS Checklist
  - Risk Factors for Developing Aspiration PNA
- Clinical Swallowing Evaluation including:
  - Oral Mech/Cranial Nerve Exam
  - PO Trials
  - Cough Strength/Airway Protection
  - Secretion management
- Instrumental Swallow Assessment
  - FEEs or MBS
- Cognitive Assessment to determine ability to:
  - Recall and follow ADL routines
  - Determine need for oral hygiene via problem solving

### OT:

- ADL Assessment/GG Score

### PT:

- Balance and Mobility Assessment

## TREATMENT IDEAS

- Use of Free Water Protocol or Ice Chip Protocol as indicated to help with oral patency
- Resistive Oral motor exercises to improve strength and ROM of musculature affecting oral health
- Use of Saliva Substitute as needed for preventing xerostomia
- Use of Sensory related swallow therapy to improve awareness of bolus and pocketed food
- Cognitive treatment from both SLP and OT focused on follow through with oral hygiene
- Use of Balance, mobility, and strength training from PT to improve tolerance of optimal positions for oral hygiene tasks
