# ONE Clinical Wound Protocol

## Purpose of Therapy

- To prevent wound development and preserve highest level of skin integrity
- To expedite or optimize wound healing
- To facilitate a OneClinical wound approach at facilities for best outcome
- To facilitate best practices for wound assessment and documentation
- To facilitate programming with options for advanced wound healing approaches

---

## Federal Tags (F-Tags)

### F 697 - Pain Management
The facility must ensure that pain management is provided to residents who require such services, consistent with professional standards of practice, the comprehensive person-centered care plan, and the residents' goals and preferences.

### F 686 - Treatment/Services to Prevent Pressure/Heal PU's
The facility must ensure that:
- A resident receives care, consistent with professional standards of practice, to prevent pressure ulcers, and does not develop pressure ulcers unless the individual's clinical condition demonstrates that they were unavoidable
- A resident with pressure ulcers receives necessary treatment and services to promote healing, prevent infection, and prevent new ulcers from developing

### F 684 - Quality of Care
Each resident must receive, and the facility must provide the necessary care and services to attain the highest practicable physical, mental, and psychosocial well-being, in accordance with the comprehensive assessment and plan of care: SKIN

**Dignity:**
- Control odor
- Bandage strike-through cannot be present when resident is in common areas

## Nursing (RN/LPN) - Comprehensive Skin Assessment

The comprehensive skin assessment should include:

- Assessing for skin integrity and areas of pressure
- Assessing for discoloration of the skin
- Variations in warmth or coolness upon palpation
- Variations in moisture
- Variations in firmness, mobility, and turgor
- Skin lesions
- Assess skin under medical devices/splints and document condition consistently
- Assess & document newly closed/healed wounds to monitor skin integrity of this delicate skin post healing (frequency of assessment determined by facility)

## Physical Therapy/Occupational Therapy (PT/OT)

### Tools & Assessments

Use of standardized/evidence-based testing, such as:

- **BWAT** - Bates-Jensen Wound Assessment Tool
- **PUSH** - Pressure Ulcer Scale for Healing
- **ABI** - Ankle Brachial Index
- Capillary Refill Time
- Rubor of Dependency vascular test
- Monofilament Semmes-Weinstein 5.07 sensory testing
- Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses
- Edema circumferential measurements and severity
- Assess general joint ROM and contracture management
- Smoking assessment & cessation education
- Assess appropriate nutrition and hydration
- Assess patient positioning: i.e. bed/mattress, W/C modifications, cushion/seating system

### Goal Examples

#### Wound Healing Goals:
- Patient will decrease L heel wound length, width, and depth by 0.5 cm to demonstrate progression towards epithelialization by X date and to allow for SPV level gait with RW.
- Patient will decrease L palm wound pain at rest from 5 to 3 to demonstrate wound progression and to promote overall patient well-being by X date to allow for independence when eating with a fork.
- L distal tibial wound will have minimal serosanguinous drainage as a demonstration of wound healing by X date to allow for independent donning/doffing of sock.
- Pt will decrease the area of R sacral wound from current XXcm² to XXcm² within 2 weeks in order to increase ability to move in/out bed comfortably.
- Pt will increase viable tissue in R sacral wound bed from current 50% to 80% granulation tissue within 2 weeks in order to reduce risk of infection.
- Pt will decrease pain noted in R sacral wound from current 5/10 to 2/10 using NRS within 2 weeks in order to improve general mobility.
- Pt will decrease undermining on R sacral wound at 12 o'clock by current 2 cm to 1 cm within 1 week in order to reduce infection risk.
- Pt will decrease tunneling on R sacral wound at 3 o'clock by current 3 cm to 1 cm within 2 weeks.

#### Functional Goals:
- Pt will have 100% understanding and return demonstration of donning PRAFO off-loading positioning device for R heel to provide pressure relief and prevent further skin breakdown in 1 week.
- Pt will demonstrate ability to provide pressure relief to L ischial tuberosity wound in WC with min assist to allow for improved wound healing in 2 weeks.
- Pt will engage in rolling in bed with mod assist in order to assist with pressure relief and ADLs in 2 weeks.
- Pt will engage in sit to stand with mod assist to improve ability to offload sacral wound and reduce risk for further skin breakdown in 2 weeks.
- Pt will tolerate sitting up in wheelchair x 1 hour with necessary w/c positioning devices without c/o pain and without skin breakdown in 2 weeks.
- Patient will demonstrate compliance wearing Darco shoe for heel offloading of L heel wound 3/3 times in 1 week to facilitate healing.

## MEASURE Acronym for Documentation

Elements for documentation and assessment use the **MEASURE** acronym, as following:

- **M** - Proper wound measurement using clock method, and accurate description of wound location
- **E** - Documentation of exudate amount/quality
- **A** - Documentation of appearance of wound/peri-wound, including tissue type, and percentage
- **S** - Documentation of suffering/pain with appropriate tools such as VAS or Wong-Baker Faces scale
- **U** - Documentation of undermining and tunneling, including location
- **R** - Documentation of wound bed reassessment, including prognosis for healing, barriers to progress, and effectiveness of interventions
- **E** - Documentation to include description of wound edges as attached, unattached, rolled (epibole), etc.
