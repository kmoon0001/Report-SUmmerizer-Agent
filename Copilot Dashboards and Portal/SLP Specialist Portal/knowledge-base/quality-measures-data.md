# Quality Measures

- PT directly reduces falls through balance training, gait retraining, assistive device prescription, and HEP
- Requires trained PT for prescription and 4 follow-up visits
- STEADI Fall Prevention Program (CDC)
- Improves balance and lower extremity strength
- Specific program prescribed with frequency and duration
- Outcome measure baseline and progress scores
- Pelvic Floor Muscle Training (PFMT)
- Level I evidence for stress, urge, and mixed UI
- Scheduled voiding with progressive delay intervals (15–30 min increments). Urge suppression: pelvic floor contraction, distraction, deep breathing.
- Reduces urge incontinence episodes by 57%
- Until continent voiding established (goal 48–72h)
- Newman DK, et al. ICI-ICS 6th ed. 2017.
- PT manages functional constipation and fecal incontinence through pelvic floor retraining, abdominal massage, and mobility programs
- Pelvic Floor Retraining for Dyssynergia
- Rao et al. Gastroenterology 2010
- Clockwise abdominal massage following colon anatomy (ascending → transverse → descending). I-love-U pattern. 15 minutes daily. Gentle pressure, 2–3 cm depth.
- Reduces constipation symptoms and laxative use
- Lämås et al. Int J Nursing Studies 2009
- Coordination with nursing for timed toileting schedule
- PT prevents pressure injuries through positioning programs, mobility progression, and seating/equipment prescription
- Reduces trochanteric and sacral pressure
- BWAT score if injury present
- Braden score with subscale breakdown
- PT improves functional mobility, reducing 30-day readmission risk and optimizing PDPM Section GG scores
- Accurate baseline documentation of all Section GG items at admission. Goal: document realistic but ambitious discharge functional goals. Items: eating, oral hygiene, toileting, dressing, transfers, ambulation, stairs.
- Section GG functional scores (MDS 3.0): admission vs discharge
- Section GG scores at admission and discharge
- Functional goals with measurable outcomes
- PTs billing Medicare Part B ≥$90K/year or ≥200 Medicare patients are eligible/required. 2026 performance affects 2028 payment adjustment (±9%)
- Per individual MIPS measure requirements
- Validated functional tool used at start and end of care
- Fall risk screening tool used (TUG, BBS, or 4-Stage Balance Test)
- Patient identified as at risk for falls
- Fall prevention plan documented in medical record
- List includes all medications (Rx, OTC, supplements)
- Plan of care for UI documented
- Validated functional outcome tool selected
- Baseline score documented at start of care
- Pain assessment completed using validated scale
- Refer to CMS MIPS measure specification for documentation requirements
