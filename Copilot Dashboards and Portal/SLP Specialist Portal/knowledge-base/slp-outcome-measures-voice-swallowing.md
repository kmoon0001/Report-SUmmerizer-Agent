# Slp Outcome Measures Voice Swallowing

- Standardized perceptual tool for describing vocal quality (Overall Severity, Roughness, Breathiness, Strain, Pitch, Loudness).
- Kempster, G. B., et al. (2009). Consensus Auditory-Perceptual Evaluation of Voice: Development of a Standardized Clinical Protocol. American Journal of Speech-Language Pathology, 18(2), 124-132.
- Rosen, C. A., et al. (2004). Development and validation of the Voice Handicap Index-10. The Laryngoscope, 114(9), 1549-1556.
- Belafsky, P. C., et al. (2002). Validity and reliability of the reflux symptom index (RSI). Journal of Voice, 16(2), 274-277.
- Belafsky, P. C., et al. (2008). Validity and reliability of the Eating Assessment Tool (EAT-10). Annals of Otology, Rhinology & Laryngology, 117(12), 919-924.
- Mann Assessment of Swallowing Ability
- Structured, reliable, and standardized clinical bedside assessment for screening and evaluation of swallowing impairment and aspiration risk in first-time stroke patients.
- Trapl, M., et al. (2007). Gugging Swallowing Screen: a new graded bedside screen for dysphagia in stroke patients. Stroke, 38(3), 1038-1040.
- Clinical scale for tracking food and liquid intake of stroke patients, mapping to the whole spectrum of oral and non-oral intake.
- Clinical observation and diet recommendation correlation
- Crary, M. A., et al. (2005). The Functional Oral Intake Scale: a measure of oral intake in stroke patients. Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation, 86(8), 1516-1520.
- Performance Status Scale for Head and Neck Cancer
- List, M. A., et al. (1996). Performance status scale for head and neck cancer patients revisited. Cancer, 77(11), 2294-2301.
- Dynamic Imaging Grade of Swallowing Toxicity
- Standardized grading system for dysphagia identified during VFSS/MBSS, focusing on pharyngeal residue and laryngeal penetration/aspiration.
- Starmer, H. M., et al. (2016). Dynamic imaging grade of swallowing toxicity (DIGEST): A scale for standardizing reports of radiation-induced dysphagia. Head & Neck, 38(S1), E1880-E1887.
