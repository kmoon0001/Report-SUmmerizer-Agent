# SNF Command Center Privacy Notice And Consent Requirements

## Privacy Notice Requirements

- Explain what categories of data the system processes, including PHI, operational data, user identity, and workflow metadata.
- Explain the purpose of processing: care coordination, safety monitoring, operational improvement, quality reporting, and approved documentation support.
- Explain that the assistant and dashboards augment staff decision-making and do not replace clinical judgment.
- Describe audit logging, access controls, retention, and monitoring practices.
- Explain how exported summaries, alerts, and reports are protected with labels, access controls, and DLP where configured.

## Consent And Authorization Requirements

- Respect patient consent flags for family sharing, research use, image use, and other special disclosure restrictions.
- Require explicit consent for voice capture where facility policy or law requires it.
- Require explicit consent or approved clinical basis for wound photos or other identifiable images.
- Track consent source, effective date, expiration or revocation, and applicable scope.
- Ensure family communication views and exports check consent and authorized recipient status before disclosure.

## Minimum Necessary Rules

- Limit displayed or exported data to the role-appropriate minimum necessary set.
- Suppress highly restricted categories unless the user has explicit authorization.
- Mask or de-identify data in testing, demos, and analytics use cases wherever possible.

## Audit And Retention Requirements

- Log access to PHI-bearing reports, drillthrough pages, exports, and workflow actions.
- Retain audit records according to facility policy and legal requirements.
- Record break-glass access separately for retrospective privacy review.

## Special Cases

- Behavioral health, substance use, genetic data, and other specially protected data need stronger policy checks before exposure.
- Research and quality-improvement uses should follow the organization’s compliance and IRB rules.
- External sharing should require approval, labeling, logging, and policy enforcement.
