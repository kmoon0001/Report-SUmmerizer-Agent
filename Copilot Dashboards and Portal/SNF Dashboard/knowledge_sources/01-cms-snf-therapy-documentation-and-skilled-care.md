# CMS SNF Therapy Documentation and Skilled Care Requirements

**Source:** CMS Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, Chapter 8 (Coverage of Extended Care Services), Chapter 15 (Covered Medical and Other Health Services), and CMS SNF Therapy Documentation Guidance
**Scope:** Skilled nursing facility physical therapy (PT), occupational therapy (OT), and speech-language pathology (SLP) documentation requirements for Medicare Part A and Part B coverage

---

## Skilled Care Criteria — When Therapy Is Covered

Therapy services are covered only when they meet all three conditions:
1. **Medically necessary** — The service must be appropriate for the patient's medical condition and reasonable given the patient's goals.
2. **Requires the skills of a therapist** — The service must require the judgment and knowledge of a licensed therapist; it cannot be provided safely by a non-skilled person.
3. **Safe and effective delivery** — The service must be safely and effectively provided by a qualified therapist.

**Key principle:** Coverage is based on whether the patient needs skilled care, not on whether the patient is making progress. Maintenance-level skilled care is covered if the skills of a therapist are required to safely perform or supervise the service.

---

## Plan of Care (POC) — Required Elements

Every patient receiving therapy must have a plan of care that includes:
- **Date of onset** of the condition being treated
- **Diagnosis** and relevant ICD-10 codes
- **Long-term functional goals** written in measurable terms with a target timeframe
- **Short-term goals** progressing toward the long-term goal
- **Specific interventions** and projected frequency and duration
- **Therapist signature and credentials** with date of certification
- **Physician/NPP certification or recertification** where applicable (Part A)

---

## Progress Notes — Required Documentation Elements

Progress notes must be written with sufficient detail to support continued coverage. Required elements:
- **Dated, signed entry** by the treating therapist or qualified professional
- **Changes in clinical status** from prior documented state
- **Patient response to treatment** — objective measures where possible
- **Progress toward short-term and long-term goals** with quantifiable data
- **Changes to the plan of care** and rationale
- **Estimate of when the goal will be reached** or explanation if goal is modified

**Documentation frequency:** At minimum, one clinically meaningful progress note per ten treatment days.

---

## Functional Justification

Medicare requires that therapy documentation justify the functional impact of the condition being treated:
- Use standardized outcome measures where applicable (e.g., FIM scores, BERG Balance, MMSE, SLUMS, FOIS, percent accuracy, repetitions with cues)
- State baseline, current, and goal status using numeric values
- Document how the functional limitation impacts the patient's ability to perform activities of daily living (ADLs) or daily functions
- Avoid vague language — use "Patient requires maximum assistance of 2 for bed mobility" rather than "patient is weak"

---

## PDPM Considerations

Under the Patient Driven Payment Model (PDPM), therapy documentation also supports:
- Classification into therapy payment components (PT, OT, SLP)
- Supporting the accuracy of resident classification (MDS Section GG, Section I, Section J)
- Capturing minutes of treatment delivered by discipline for therapy volume tracking
- Documenting the reason for any clinical change that affects classification

---

## Documentation Audit Risk Areas (MAC / RAC Focus)

Per MAC guidance and recovery audit contractor (RAC) patterns, the highest-risk documentation gaps include:
- Missing physician certification or recertification for Part A therapy
- Skilled justification absent from progress notes — notes that read as routine without describing clinical complexity
- Goals that are vague, not measurable, or not updated after the target date
- Treatment notes that do not match the plan of care intensities
- Services provided by therapy aides or non-qualified persons without the direct supervision designation and therapist skill involvement documented

---

## Daily Treatment Log Requirements

For each session, the treating therapist or qualified clinician must document:
- Date and session duration
- Discipline and provider name and credentials
- Modalities and interventions delivered during the session
- Patient's response to treatment and performance data
- Attendance, patient tolerance, and any safety concerns
- Whether services were provided by the therapist personally or under supervision

---

## Skilled Maintenance Documentation

If a patient requires ongoing therapy to prevent deterioration and the skills of a therapist are needed for safety:
- Document the specific skilled task the therapist must perform (e.g., "Patient requires hands-on facilitation due to fall risk during ambulation")
- Document the patient's baseline functional status and why non-skilled staff cannot safely provide this service
- Do not stop documenting skilled justification just because the patient is in a maintenance phase
