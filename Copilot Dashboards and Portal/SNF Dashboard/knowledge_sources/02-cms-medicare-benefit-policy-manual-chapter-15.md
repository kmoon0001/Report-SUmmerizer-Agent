# CMS Medicare Benefit Policy Manual — Chapter 8 SNF Coverage Rules

**Source:** CMS Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, Chapter 8 — Coverage of Extended Care Services (SNF); CMS Internet-Only Manual (IOM) Publication 100-02
**Scope:** Medicare Part A SNF benefit eligibility and coverage criteria for post-acute skilled nursing facility stays

---

## SNF Benefit Eligibility — Core Requirements

Medicare Part A covers SNF stays only when all of the following requirements are met:

### 1. Qualifying Hospital Stay (3-Midnight Rule)
- The patient must have a medically necessary **inpatient hospital stay of at least 3 consecutive midnights** immediately before SNF admission
- Observation stays do NOT count toward the 3-midnight requirement
- The 3-midnight rule applies to the medically necessary period — if a patient is admitted under observation and later converted to inpatient, only inpatient midnights count

### 2. Direct Transfer Timing
- SNF admission must occur within **30 calendar days** of discharge from the qualifying hospital
- Exceptions may apply for medically necessary delays — document the reason for delayed admission if applicable

### 3. Skilled Care Requirement
- The patient must require **skilled nursing or skilled rehabilitation services** on a daily basis that:
  - Can only be provided by or under the supervision of licensed professionals
  - Are required 7 days per week for skilled nursing, or 5 days per week for skilled therapy
- **Daily skilled care** is evaluated based on the patient's actual clinical need, not solely on conditions that improve

### 4. Medicare-Certified SNF
- The SNF must be Medicare-certified and accepting Medicare assignment for the admission

---

## Coverage Duration — Part A Benefit Periods

| Days | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Days 1–20 | 100% covered (no coinsurance) |
| Days 21–100 | Patient pays daily coinsurance (adjusted annually by CMS) |
| After Day 100 | Medicare Part A coverage ends; patient transitions to other payment sources |

**Benefit period resets** after 60 consecutive days without a qualifying inpatient hospital or SNF level of care.

---

## Level of Care — Daily Skilled Care Standard

CMS defines skilled care as services that:
- **Require the skills, abilities, and knowledge** of a registered nurse (RN), licensed practical nurse (LPN/LVN), or qualified therapist (PT, OT, SLP)
- Are **ordered by a physician or NPP** and performed under a plan of care
- **Cannot be provided safely or effectively by non-skilled personnel**

Examples of covered skilled nursing services:
- IV medication administration, tube feeding, complex wound care, post-surgical monitoring, and diabetic management requiring skilled assessment
- Observation and monitoring of a patient in an unstable condition that requires skilled clinical judgment

Examples of covered skilled therapy services:
- PT: therapeutic exercises requiring clinical adaptation, gait training after neurological or orthopedic event
- OT: ADL retraining for patients with cognitive or functional decline after acute medical event
- SLP: dysphagia evaluation and treatment, cognitive-communication therapy

---

## Medical Necessity and Documentation

Coverage requires that documentation demonstrate:
1. **The patient's medical condition** necessitating skilled care
2. **The skilled interventions** planned and delivered
3. **The clinical rationale** for each intervention
4. **Response to treatment** with measurable outcome data
5. **Physician certification** of the plan of care at start of care and periodically thereafter

**RAC/MAC audit risk:** The highest denials occur when documentation does not justify skilled care at the level billed, or when notes are generic without patient-specific clinical detail.

---

## Discharge Planning Obligations

SNFs must initiate discharge planning at admission:
- Identify the anticipated discharge destination
- Involve the patient and family in goal-setting
- Coordinate with receiving providers for continuity of care
- Document the discharge plan and any updates in the medical record
- CMS IMPACT Act requirements: standardized data reporting at discharge for interoperability

---

## Non-Covered SNF Situations

Medicare Part A does **not** cover:
- Custodial care (assistance with ADLs that does not require skilled care)
- SNF admission without a qualifying hospital stay
- Services not ordered by a physician or NPP
- Experimental or investigational services
- Room and board when no skilled care is required
