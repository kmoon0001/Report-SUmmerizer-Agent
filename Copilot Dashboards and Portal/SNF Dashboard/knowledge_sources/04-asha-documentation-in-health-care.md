# ASHA Documentation Standards for Speech-Language Pathology in Health Care Settings

**Source:** American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) — Documentation in Health Care Settings; ASHA Guidelines for Speech-Language Pathology Services in Healthcare; ASHA Practice Documents and Scope of Practice in SLP (2016, updated)
**Scope:** SLP documentation requirements in skilled nursing facility and post-acute care settings, including Medicare compliance for SLP services

---

## Overview — ASHA Documentation Principles

ASHA documentation standards define the minimum expectations for ethical, legally defensible, and clinically valid SLP records. In SNF settings, these standards must align with CMS Medicare requirements to support coverage of SLP services.

---

## Evaluation Documentation Requirements

### Initial SLP Evaluation Must Include:
- **Referral source and reason for referral**
- **Medical and social history** relevant to communication or swallowing
- **Hearing status** (screening at minimum)
- **Standardized and non-standardized assessment results** with scores and norms as applicable
- **Communication status** — expressive, receptive, pragmatic, cognitive-linguistic
- **Swallowing status** — clinical swallow evaluation findings; MBSS or FEES results if applicable
- **Diagnosis** in SLP-specific terms linked to the underlying medical condition
- **Severity rating** — mild, moderate, severe, profound with functional impact described
- **Prognosis** — based on medical stability, cognition, support system, and response to treatment
- **Recommended treatment plan** with measurable goals, frequency, duration, and rationale
- **FOIS (Functional Oral Intake Scale) level** if dysphagia is present
- **Clinician signature and date** with credentials (CCC-SLP, CFY-SLP, SLPA supervision note if applicable)

---

## Functional Limitation Documentation

ASHA and CMS both require SLP documentation to support the **functional impact** of the communication or swallowing disorder:

- Link the impairment to specific functional barriers: unable to make needs known, unsafe for oral intake, cannot follow multi-step instructions, unable to communicate pain or discomfort
- Quantify the limitation whenever possible: "Patient requires moderate verbal cues for 4-step commands with 60% accuracy on standardized testing (RBANS)"
- Avoid clinical jargon without explanation — documentation must be interpretable by a non-SLP reviewer (CMS contractor, RAC auditor)

---

## Treatment Note Requirements

Each treatment session note must include:
- Date, session duration, and service type (individual vs. group, concurrent, co-treatment)
- Specific treatment targets and procedures addressed
- Objective performance data (percent accuracy, cue level, repetitions, response latency, FOIS level, PO trial outcomes)
- Progress toward short-term and long-term goals with numeric comparison to baseline
- Clinical decisions made during the session (e.g., diet level change, strategy modification, caregiver training provided)
- Patient and caregiver response to training
- Safety observations and plan of care modifications
- Signature with credentials and date

---

## Medicare Coverage for SLP in SNF

SLP services are covered under Medicare Part A and Part B when:
1. The services are **medically necessary** for the patient's diagnosis and functional status
2. The services **require the skills of a qualified SLP** — they cannot be performed safely by a non-skilled assistant
3. Services are **ordered by a physician or NPP** under an appropriate plan of care
4. Documentation supports the **complexity and clinical decision-making** involved

**Covered SLP conditions in SNF:** Aphasia, dysarthria, apraxia, cognitive-communication disorders, dysphagia, voice disorders, fluency disorders, and language processing disorders related to neurological or medical events

---

## Dysphagia-Specific Documentation

Dysphagia is the highest-risk area for SLP documentation review in SNF settings:
- **Clinical Swallow Evaluation (CSE)** must document bedside findings, current diet level (using IDDSI or NMES staging), oral phase vs. pharyngeal findings, and clinical risk level
- **Instrumental assessment referral** should be documented with clinical rationale (why MBSS or FEES is or is not indicated)
- **Diet recommendations** must be documented with the clinical basis and communicated to nursing, dietary, and the physician
- **Aspiration risk** must be documented when identified, including the management strategy (modified diet, compensatory strategies, thickened liquids)
- **Family and staff training** for dysphagia management must be documented with who was trained and what was covered

---

## Discharge and Transition of Care Documentation

At discharge from SLP services:
- Summarize the functional status at discharge vs. admission using objective measures
- Document goal outcomes (met, partially met, not met with reason)
- Document the home program or transition plan including compensatory strategies, diet modifications, and caregiver instructions
- Communicate swallowing and communication status to the receiving provider using standardized terminology
- Document any unresolved clinical concerns requiring follow-up
