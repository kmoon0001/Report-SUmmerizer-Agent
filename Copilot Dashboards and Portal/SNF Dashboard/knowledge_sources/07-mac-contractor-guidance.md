# MAC Contractor Guidance — SNF Documentation Audit Focus Areas

**Source:** Noridian Healthcare Solutions (MAC Jurisdiction F and J5); CGS Administrators (Jurisdiction 15); WPS Government Health Administrators; Palmetto GBA — publicly published SNF Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs), Coverage Determinations, and Targeted Probe and Educate (TPE) audit findings
**Scope:** Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) audit patterns, denial triggers, and documentation must-haves for SNF providers

---

## Overview — What MACs Look For

Medicare Administrative Contractors conduct pre-payment and post-payment reviews of SNF claims. Their highest-priority audit targets are SNF Part A therapy services, skilled nursing services, and extended-stay claims. Understanding MAC focus areas allows providers to self-audit proactively before external review.

---

## Top MAC Denial Reasons for SNF Claims

Based on published MAC guidance and Targeted Probe and Educate (TPE) findings:

### 1. Absence of Skilled Care Justification
- The documentation describes what was done to the patient but not **why it required a skilled clinician**
- Notes that read as task-completion logs ("patient ambulated 50 feet") without indicating the clinical complexity, clinical decisions made, or the skilled judgment required
- **Fix:** Every note must answer the question: "Why couldn't a non-skilled person have provided this service safely?"

### 2. Missing or Defective Physician Certification
- Certification document is unsigned, undated, or refers to the wrong episode period
- Recertification was late (not completed by the due date in the 30-day window)
- Certification does not include the patient's medical condition and need for skilled care
- **Fix:** Track certification and recertification due dates in your scheduling or EMR system. Never bill without a completed certification.

### 3. Functional Goals That Are Not Measurable
- Goals written as "patient will improve" or "patient will return to prior level of function" without quantifying the baseline, target, and timeframe
- Goals that are not updated after the target date passes
- **Fix:** Use SMART goals — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Always include a numeric baseline and target.

### 4. Documentation Does Not Reflect 7-Day Skilled Nursing Requirement
- Skilled nursing notes for days without treatment or monitoring activity
- Notes that are generic or copied without reflecting the patient's actual status on that day
- **Fix:** Skilled nursing notes must reflect the patient's response to care on the specific date of service, including any assessments, clinical decisions, and nursing interventions that required RN or LPN judgment.

### 5. Therapy Minutes Do Not Match Documented Content
- The billed therapy code implies a certain duration of treatment, but the documented session does not support that time
- Co-treatment time counted twice without proper documentation
- **Fix:** Document exact minutes per session. If a session is split between skilled treatment and education or equipment training, document and account for each segment.

### 6. Lack of Medical Necessity for Extended Stay
- Patient has plateaued functionally but skilled care rationale is not updated to reflect maintenance or complex monitoring
- **Fix:** When a patient plateaus, shift the documentation rationale to maintenance skilled care (if applicable) — document why the skills of a therapist or nurse are still required to maintain the patient's level of function safely.

## High-Risk LCD Areas for SNF

MACs use Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs) to define coverage criteria. Current high-risk LCD areas for SNF:

| LCD Area | Risk Level | Common Finding |
|---|---|---|
| PT services | High | Inadequate skilled justification for gait training |
| OT services | High | ADL documentation lacks assistance-level specificity |
| SLP-dysphagia | High | No instrumental assessment reference; missing FOIS level |
| Skilled nursing — wound care | Medium | Missing wound measurement documentation |
| Skilled nursing — IV therapy | Medium | Missing documentation of skilled monitoring need |
| Skilled nursing — tube feeding | Medium | No documentation of clinical complexity or training need |

## Targeted Probe and Educate (TPE) — What to Expect

If your facility is selected for TPE:
1. The MAC will request records for a sample of claims (typically 20–40 claims)
2. You have the opportunity to submit a written response and schedule an education session
3. If error rates remain high after two review rounds, a third round occurs — continued high error rates may lead to prepayment review
4. TPE is educational in intent; providers who engage and correct patterns typically exit the probe program

**Preparation:** Conduct a proactive internal audit using the MAC's LCD requirements as your checklist before external review occurs.

## Documentation Red Flags — Self-Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to identify documentation that is likely to be denied:

- [ ] Does every note identify a skilled intervention by name?
- [ ] Does every note include a clinical rationale for why a skilled clinician was required?
- [ ] Are all goals written with measurable baselines and target values?
- [ ] Are all goals updated when the target date passes?
- [ ] Is the physician certification on file and completed before billing?
- [ ] Are all progress notes signed with credentials and dated on the date of service?
- [ ] Do billed minutes match documented session duration?
- [ ] Is there a daily skilled nursing note for every day a Part A claim is billed?
- [ ] Does the clinical record support every level of care billed (RUG level or PDPM component)?

## Post-Payment Review — Responding to an Audit

If you receive an Additional Documentation Request (ADR):
1. Respond within the specified timeframe (typically 45 days)
2. Submit all requested records — incomplete responses default to denial
3. Include a cover letter explaining the clinical context if the records need context to be understood
4. Follow up via the MAC portal or contact number to confirm receipt
5. If denied, appeal using the appeals process (redetermination → reconsideration → ALJ → Appeals Council → Federal Court)
