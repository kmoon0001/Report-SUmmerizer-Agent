Title: Strong SLP Documentation Patterns (Qualitative Reference)

Examples of “strong” SLP documentation patterns in SNF/post-acute settings:

- Evaluation:
  - Describes prior and current communication and/or swallowing status with objective measures (e.g., intelligibility %, naming scores, comprehension levels, FOIS, diet/liquid level, instrumental study results when available).
  - Identifies key safety and participation concerns (aspiration risk, ability to follow medical instructions, communicate needs, manage meds, or participate in decisions).

- Goals:
  - Specific, measurable, and time-bound (e.g., “In 2 weeks, patient will safely consume mechanical-soft solids and nectar-thick liquids with no overt signs of aspiration during meals with supervision.”).
  - Link deficits to real-life communication or swallowing participation (family conversations, phone calls, medical discussions, mealtime safety).

- Daily/progress notes:
  - Show skilled SLP work (strategy training, cueing hierarchy, compensatory techniques, AAC setup and coaching, dysphagia management decisions).
  - Use objective/standardized or structured observations to show change (score shifts, diet upgrades, fewer aspiration signs, more effective communication in specific contexts).
  - Document caregiver/staff education for safe feeding, supported communication, and monitoring for red-flag signs.

- Discharge:
  - Compare baseline vs discharge communication/swallowing using similar measures.
  - Clearly state goal status and rationale (met, partially met, not met).
  - Provide specific recommendations for ongoing support, diet level, strategies, and safety precautions.

Use these patterns as qualitative “good examples” when judging SLP strengths, gaps, and suggested improvements. Do not copy exact wording; keep examples paraphrased and illustrative only.
