Strong OT Documentation Patterns (Qualitative Reference)

Examples of “strong” OT documentation patterns in SNF/post-acute settings:

- Evaluation:
  - Describes prior and current independence for ADLs/IADLs (bathing, dressing, toileting, grooming, meal prep, meds, housekeeping, money management).
  - Uses clear assistance levels (dependent/max/mod/min/supervision/independent) tied to specific tasks.
  - Identifies cognitive, sensory, visual-perceptual, and environmental factors that affect safety and performance.

- Goals:
  - Functional, measurable, and time-bound (e.g., “In 3 weeks, patient will complete upper-body dressing with setup only, seated, using adaptive techniques, to increase self-care independence.”).
  - Connect impairments and environmental barriers to meaningful roles (home management, caregiving, community participation).

- Daily/progress notes:
  - Describe skilled OT reasoning (why specific activities, strategies, or environmental modifications were chosen and progressed).
  - Show objective change in ADL/IADL performance or efficiency (less assist, fewer cues, better safety, improved tolerance).
  - Document caregiver/staff training on techniques, equipment, and safety routines when needed.

- Discharge:
  - Compare baseline vs discharge ADL/IADL status using similar scales.
  - Clarify which goals were met/partially met/not met, and why.
  - Outline carryover recommendations (home program, equipment, supervision level) aligned with safety.

Use these patterns as qualitative “good examples” when judging OT strengths, gaps, and suggested improvements. Do not copy exact wording; keep examples paraphrased and illustrative only.
