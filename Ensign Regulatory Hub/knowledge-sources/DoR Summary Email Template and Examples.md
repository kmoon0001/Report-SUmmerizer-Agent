# DoR Summary Email Template and Examples

## Purpose

This document provides the templates and audience-specific formatting rules the SimpleLTC QM Coach agent uses when generating Director of Rehab (DoR) summary emails. The DoR summary consolidates QM performance across multiple facilities into a single communication for therapy leadership.

## Authoritative Sources

- CMS Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, Pub. 100-02, Ch. 15, §220.2
- CMS QAPI requirements at 42 CFR §483.75
- Internal organizational communication standards for clinical reporting

## Email Structure

Every DoR summary email follows this structure:

### Header Block
```
Subject: QM Performance Summary — [Region/Market Name] — [Reporting Period]
To: [DoR Name], [Regional Therapy Consultant]
CC: [Administrator(s) as applicable]
Date: [Generated Date]
Generated By: SimpleLTC QM Coach V2 (AI-generated decision support)
```

### Executive Summary (2-3 sentences)
- Overall performance trend across all facilities in scope
- Number of facilities reviewed
- Count of High, Moderate, and Low concern QMs identified

### Facility Performance Table
```
| Facility | Top Concern QM | Rate | vs. National Avg | Trend | Concern Level |
|----------|---------------|------|-------------------|-------|---------------|
| [Name]   | [QM Name]     | [%]  | [+/- pts]         | [↑↓→] | [H/M/L]      |
```

### Priority Actions by Facility
For each facility with High or Moderate concerns:
- Facility name
- QM domain and current rate
- Recommended 7-day action (one sentence)
- Owner assignment

### Wins and Improvements
- Facilities showing improvement trends
- QMs that moved below benchmark
- Successful interventions worth replicating

### Closing
```
⚠️ This summary is AI-generated decision support. All recommendations require
licensed clinician review before implementation.

Next review scheduled: [Date]
```

## Audience-Specific Formatting

### For DoR (Primary Audience)
- Lead with therapy-specific action items
- Include therapy minutes context where relevant
- Reference ONE Clinical Protocols by name
- Focus on what the DoR needs to do this week
- Keep clinical detail moderate — the DoR knows the clinical context

### For Regional Therapy Consultant
- Lead with cross-facility trends and patterns
- Highlight facilities that need on-site support
- Include benchmark comparisons
- Focus on systemic issues vs. individual facility issues
- Include regulatory risk context (F-tag exposure)

### For Administrator
- Lead with Five-Star rating impact
- Minimize clinical jargon
- Focus on resource needs and timeline
- Include escalation status
- Keep to one page equivalent

### For IDT / Nursing Leadership
- Lead with nursing-specific action items
- Include MDS coding accuracy context
- Reference care plan update needs
- Focus on therapy-nursing collaboration points
- Include restorative nursing program status

## Example: Multi-Facility DoR Summary

```
Subject: QM Performance Summary — Southern California Region — Q1 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Reviewed 5 facilities for Q1 2026. Two facilities flagged High concern
(Sea Cliff for falls, Palm Terrace for ADL decline). Three facilities
are stable or improving. Overall regional fall rate trending upward and
requires coordinated response.

FACILITY PERFORMANCE
| Facility      | Top Concern QM          | Rate  | vs. Natl | Trend | Level |
|---------------|------------------------|-------|----------|-------|-------|
| Sea Cliff     | Falls w/ Major Injury  | 5.2%  | +1.8 pts | ↑     | High  |
| Palm Terrace  | ADL Decline            | 22.1% | +4.1 pts | ↑     | High  |
| Victoria      | Antipsychotic Use      | 15.8% | +0.8 pts | →     | Mod   |
| St. Catherine | Pressure Ulcers (SS)   | 1.1%  | -0.4 pts | ↓     | Low   |
| Mainplace     | All QMs                | —     | Below avg| →     | Low   |

PRIORITY ACTIONS

Sea Cliff — Falls with Major Injury (5.2%, High)
- 7-Day: Initiate post-fall huddle protocol review; complete fall risk
  reassessment for all residents with falls in last 30 days
- Owner: DoR + DON
- Protocol: ONE Clinical Protocol for Fall Prevention

Palm Terrace — ADL Decline (22.1%, High)
- 7-Day: Audit restorative nursing program participation rates; identify
  residents discharged from therapy without restorative handoff
- Owner: DoR
- Protocol: ONE Clinical Protocol for Contracture Management
- Jimmo Note: Review long-stay residents for maintenance therapy eligibility

Victoria — Antipsychotic Use (15.8%, Moderate)
- 30-Day: Coordinate with pharmacy consultant on GDR candidates; initiate
  behavioral management assessment for top 5 antipsychotic users
- Owner: DoR + DON + Pharmacy
- Protocol: ONE Clinical Protocol for Behavior Management

WINS
- St. Catherine: Pressure ulcer rate improved from 1.8% to 1.1% after
  implementing seating/positioning program in Q4 2025
- Mainplace: All QMs below national average for 3rd consecutive quarter

⚠️ This summary is AI-generated decision support. All recommendations
require licensed clinician review before implementation.

Next review scheduled: April 15, 2026
```

## Example: Single-Facility DoR Summary

```
Subject: QM Performance Summary — Sea Cliff — March 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Sea Cliff shows a concerning upward trend in falls with major injury
(5.2%, up from 3.1% in Q4 2025). ADL decline is stable but above
target. All other QMs are within acceptable range.

QM SNAPSHOT
| QM Domain                | Rate  | Target | vs. Natl | Trend |
|--------------------------|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| Falls w/ Major Injury    | 5.2%  | ≤2.5%  | +1.8 pts | ↑↑    |
| ADL Decline              | 16.3% | ≤12%   | +1.3 pts | →     |
| Pressure Ulcers (SS)     | 0.8%  | ≤1%    | -0.7 pts | ↓     |
| Antipsychotic Use        | 11.2% | ≤12%   | -2.8 pts | ↓     |
| Incontinence (Low-Risk)  | 42.1% | ≤40%   | -2.9 pts | →     |

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (This Week)
1. Complete fall risk reassessment for all residents with falls in
   last 30 days — Owner: Therapy team
2. Review environmental safety rounds compliance — Owner: DON
3. Audit medication profiles for fall-risk medications — Owner: Pharmacy
4. Schedule IDT fall prevention huddle — Owner: DoR

30-DAY ACTIONS
1. Implement standardized post-fall investigation protocol
2. Train CNAs on fall prevention during ADL assistance
3. Evaluate night shift fall prevention coverage

⚠️ HITL Required: This summary requires licensed clinician review.
```

## How the Agent Uses This Document

When generating DoR summary emails:
1. Determine the audience from the user's request context
2. Apply the audience-specific formatting rules
3. Follow the email structure exactly
4. Include the facility performance table for multi-facility summaries
5. Always include the HITL disclaimer
6. Reference specific ONE Clinical Protocols and CMS standards
7. Assign owners from the roles defined in the Action Plan SOP
