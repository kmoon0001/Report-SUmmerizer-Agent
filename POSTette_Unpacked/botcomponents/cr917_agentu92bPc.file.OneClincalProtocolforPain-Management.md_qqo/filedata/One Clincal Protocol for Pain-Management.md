## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

- To identify most appropriate ways of communicating pain to care providers
- To address pain in a non-pharmacological approach
- To effectively monitor pain and pain response as it affects functional performance

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### PT/OT Goals:

- Patient will achieve __ degrees of shoulder ROM with 2/10 report of pain.
- Pt will reduce low back pain from 8 to 3/10 with use of modalities during OOB functional activities.
- Patient will decrease need for pain medication to PRN with use of therapeutic interventions with pain level of 1-3/10 reported during ADLs/mobility.
- Patient will utilize established pain rating scale during all episodes of care with 100% carryover.
- Patient will be able to demonstrate clear communication of pain 5/5 occasions using established communication methods.
- Patient will utilize pain assessment strategies with trained caregiver during ADL performance for 5/5 occasions.
- The patient will improve response to adapted pain scale with trained caregiver to 75% of the time to improve communication of pain and medical needs.

### ST Goals:

- The patient will answer simple yes/no questions via head nod, thumbs up/down, or visual aid with 60% accuracy with trained caregiver to improve ability to participate in meaningful interactions, communicate pain to trained caregivers, and participate in ADLs.
- The patient will display appropriate word finding during structured and unstructured tasks with 80% to improve ability to participate in meaningful interactions, communicate pain to trained caregivers, and participate in facility activities.
- The patient will make a binary choice on 3/5 opportunities to communicate preferences about the care and activities within a structured supportive to decrease behavioral outburst episodes relating to pain and resistance to care.

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### PCC Assessments:

**Visual Analogue Scale** - The intensity of pain is rated on a line marked from no pain at one end, to as bad as it could possibly be at the other end. Can be used with most patients, caution with cognitive impairments.

**The Numeric Rating Scale (NRS)** - is a tool to rate the intensity of pain on a scale from 0-10. The Verbal Numeric Rating Scale may have a limited role for communication or cognitively impaired patients.

**Faces Pain Scale** - The scale usually has six faces ranging in expression from smiling to crying, and they carry numbers from 0 to 5 which would be assigned to a pain level

**The Pain Thermometer** - Patients can simply point to a word which resembles how they are feeling at that time.
