# ONE Clinical Contracture Management Protocol

## POSSIBLE F-TAG/QMS

Each patient must receive and the facility must provide the necessary care and services to attain the highest practicable physical, mental, and psychosocial well-being, in accordance with the comprehensive assessment and plan of care.

**Range of Motion F684**

Based on comprehensive assessment of a patient, the facility must ensure that a patient who enters the facility without limited range of motion does not experience reduction in range of motion unless the patient's clinical condition demonstrates that a reduction in range of motion is unavoidable

A patient with limited range of motion receives appropriate treatment and services to increase range of motion and/or to prevent further decreases in range of motion.

**F688**

**Quality Measures**

## THERAPIES

- PT
- OT

## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

Prevent and reduce presence of contractures and improve overall quality of life and ability to engage in daily routine.

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### PT/OT Goals:

- Patient will reduce pain associated with contracture to 2/10 with use of modalities prior to manual therapy interventions.
- Pt will increase ROM at _____ (joint) by ___ degrees in order to improve wearing tolerance of prescribed splint.
- Patient will engage in hand hygiene pre and post hand splint application with Min assist to reduce risk for skin breakdown.
- Patient will tolerate _____ splint x 4 hours without s/s of skin breakdown.
- Patient will adhere to splint wearing schedule with independence utilizing visual reminders.
- Pt will be independent with self skin inspection post splint removal.
- Pt will be 100% compliant with splint application following developed wearing schedule with trained caregiver support.
- Pt will tolerate ROM and stretching prior to splint application with 100% compliance from trained caregiver support.
- Pt will increase ankle ROM to _____ in order to tolerate sit to stand with Min assist.
- Pt will increase shoulder ROM to ______ in order to improve independence with combing hair to Mod assist.
- Pt will increase elbow ROM to ______ in order to improve self feeding to Min assist.
- Pt will use a modified visual pain scale on 4/5 opportunities to communicate pain levels to caregivers.

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

- ROM assessments
- Evaluate bed and WC positioning that could contribute to contracture development and/or improvement.
- Seating evaluation as necessary. Include positioning program with thorough education of nursing staff
- Rate tone using Modified Ashworth Scale

### NEURO STRETCH TECHNIQUES:

Recommended only for patients with abnormal tone or spasticity

PROM technique which should not initiate a stretch reflex.

Designed to activate the GTO at the affected joint producing an inhibitory response.

Recommended for patients with abnormal tone or spasticity.

The Neuro Stretch technique helps specify treatment approaches by assessing the tissue and neuro aspect of a contracture
