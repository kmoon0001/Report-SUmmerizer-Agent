## THERAPIES

- OT

## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

Help our residents with dementia, cognitive impairments, and unwanted behaviors to be as safe and independent as possible with utilization of sensory strategies, activity interest/engagement, and adapted care approaches.

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### OT Goals:

- Patient will engage in activity for 10 minutes with therapist to redirect unwanted behavior for 3/3 sessions.
- Patient will engage in activity for 10 minutes with trained caregiver to redirect unwanted behavior for 3/3 sessions.
- Patient will participate in sensory diet program for 15 minutes with therapist with 100% compliance to decrease agitation and facilitate improved quality of resident care.
- Patient will participate in sensory diet program for 15 minutes with trained caregiver with 100% compliance to decrease agitation and facilitate improved quality of resident care.
- Resident will decrease pain to 3/10 with use of modalities prior to ROM to prevent contractures.
- Patient will sequence 5/5 steps to place a call to family member on cell phone, with use of visual sequence aid and moderate vanishing cues, to increase ability to make phone calls to family members to communicate daily wants/needs.
- Patient will use communication supports in a field of 4 to indicate 3 basic needs to caregivers on 4/5 opportunities in order to communicate basic needs.
- Resident will reduce negative behaviors during morning care on 4/5 sessions.
- Patient will decrease negative behaviors during activities with min verbal cues provided 4/5 sessions.
- Patient reports sleeping 7 to 8 hours per night 5/5 sessions.
- Patient reports falling back to sleep within 20 minutes if waking during night 5/5 sessions.
- Resident will engage in leisure task of choice with min (hand over and/tactile/verbal/visual) cues to facilitate engagement.
- Pt will follow visual schedule with min verbal cues to facilitate participation in facility activities.
- Pt will demonstrate orientation/navigation in the facility using predetermined visual cues with less than 25% verbal cues.
- Pt will follow (1, 2, 3) step directions with use of a visual schedule with (%) verbal cues provided.
- Pt will tolerate (tactile, auditory, gustatory, vestibular, visual, olfactory) input presented by (the therapist, a trained caregiver) for (5, 10, 15) minutes without presenting negative behaviors (%) of opportunities presented.
- Pt will engage in meaningful tasks that provide (tactile, proprioceptive, vestibular, auditory, visual, olfactory, gustatory) input for (5, 10, 15) minutes in 3/3 sessions.
- Patient will perform grooming and hygiene tasks with moderate (35-50%) verbal, visual, tactile cues for correct sequencing tasks provided by trained caregivers.

## IMPLEMENTATION

### Identify strategies to assist with recall of environmental information:

- Aides
- Spaced Retrieval techniques, etc.

### Identify effective cueing strategies to increase processing of and attention to task demands

### Identify strategies to increase patient safety within current environment

### Identify stimuli that elicits a desired communicative response

### Integrate sensory strategies into daily routines to enhance meaningful engagement with patient

### Use of environmental modifications for lighting, sounds:

- White noise machines
- Other equipment that can increase or decrease sensory stimulation a space provides

### Use of remedial interventions:

- Activities that encourage engagement of tactile, proprioceptive, and movement opportunities
- Promote motor planning, such as cooking or meal prepping activities

### Use of sensory diet programs that are individualized:

- Rocking chair
- Quiet space
- Aromatherapy
- Weighted blanket
- Stress balls
- Fidget items, etc.
- Help manage sensory modulation problems or reduce symptoms of anxiety or agitation
- Encourage participation in needed or desired activities

### Identify optimal ADL set up to facilitate function and reduce excess disability

### Modify task demands to match the cognitive level of the patient

### Optimize set-up and environment

### Train facility staff on ACL levels and individualized approaches for residents

### Provide ADL and activities prescriptions/guides for residents to facility staff

### Heart rate variability and strategies training to patients and staff

### Sleep hygiene training to patients and staff

### Pain management through modalities:

- Dry Needling
- Cupping
- Use of communication supports and visual aids to communicate pain effectively
