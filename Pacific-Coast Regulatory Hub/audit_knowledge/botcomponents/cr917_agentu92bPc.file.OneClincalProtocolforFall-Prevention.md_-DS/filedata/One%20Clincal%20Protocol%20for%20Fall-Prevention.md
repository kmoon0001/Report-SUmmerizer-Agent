## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

- Reduce environmental contributions to fall risk and maximize safety strategies.
- Improve balance reactions and stability with OOB tasks.
- Improve use of call light and enhance communications strategies to convey resident needs.
- Identify root cause of fall risks for individual residents.
- Staff training and education to ensure carryover of implemented strategies to minimize fall risk.
- Identify meaningful activities of choice to improve engagement and reduce wandering and risk of falls.

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### PT/OT Goals:

- Pt will attend to visual reminders ___/3 times to reduce independent attempts getting OOB.
- Pt will utilize call light __/3 times to request staff assist for ADL needs.
- Pt will sit unsupported at EOB 5 minutes without LOB to reduce risk of falls in room.
- Pt will improve modified functional reach to _____ inches to improve ability to reach for ADL items without falls at EOB.
- Pt will reduce 5 times sit to stand score to ____ seconds within norms for age range in order to improve strength for transfers with Min assist.
- Pt will improve transfers to ______ assist with 25% multi-modal cues with prescribed assistive device.
- Pt will identify __/3 safety hazards in room with 25% verbal cues.
- Pt will engage in standing tasks including weight shifting and reaching outside base of support with Fair+ balance to reduce LOB during functional tasks.

### ST Goals:

- Pt will utilize visual aides for call light assistance with min cues provided during transfers in 10/10 attempts in order to decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will recall 4/4 safety precautions during transfers with min cues provided in order to decrease assistance required for functional activities and decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will identify safety hazardous in room with min cues provided at 90% accuracy in order to decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will accurately sequence safety steps during transfer with min cues provided at 90% accuracy in order to decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will sustain attention to relevant task with min cues provided for a maximum duration of 5 minutes in order to follow instructions for safe ambulation and transfers to decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will accurately follow 3-step commands provided min cues at 90% accuracy in order to increase safety during functional activities and decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will accurately complete 3-4 step commands utilizing errorless learning techniques in 5/5 attempts in order to decrease risk for falls.
- Pt will identify safe footwear in specific environments provided min cues at 90% accuracy in order to decrease risk for falls.
- Patient will complete short term memory and delayed recall tasks with 80% accuracy and minimal verbal and visual cues to improve orientation, decrease fall risk, and decrease behaviors and anxiety related to forgetfulness.

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### Fall Risk Tests:

- TUG Test
- 30 Second Sit to Stand Test
- 4-Stage Balance Test
- Orthostatic Blood Pressure Test

### Strength and Muscle Performance:

- Chair Rise Test
- Getting up from Lying on the Floor Test

### Aerobic Capacity

- 6 Minute Walk Test
- Seated Step Test

### Gait, Locomotion, Balance & Vestibular:

- Berg
- Timed Up and Go (TUG)
- Functional & Modified Functional Reach Test
- Range of Motion Test
- Chair, Sit and Reach Test
- Dizziness Handicap Inventory (DHI)
- Sensory Interaction – Foam & Dome
- Falls Efficacy Scale / Confidence Index
- Dynamic Gait Index
- Physical Performance Test

### Cognitive:

- Allen Cognitive Levels
- Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA)
- Cognitive Performance Test (CPT)
- St. Louis University Mental Status Exam (SLUMS)
- Cognitive Linguistic Quick Test

### Otago Fall Prevention Program (requires certification):

- 52 Week Exercise Prescription
- Series of 17 Strength & Balance exercises

### STEADI Initiative:

Contains the three core elements of screening, assessing, and implementing interventions for fall risk reduction

## THERAPIES & INTERVENTIONS

### Sensory Impairment Intervention

- Orthotics/ Shoe Modification
- Vision correction/ Visual Tracking
- Depth perception rehabilitation

### Urinary Incontinence

- Toileting Program
- Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation/PTNM
- Assess Bathroom Set-up

### Pain management

- Modalities
- Dry Needing, Cupping

### Balance Loss/Mobility Training

- Resistance Training
- Balance activities
- Gait and agility training
- Assistive device training

### Vestibular

- Assess for BPPV
- Static and Dynamic with eyes open/closed
- Balance pad with head movements or closed eyes to isolate vestibular reactions

### Positioning Impairments

- W/C modifications, cushion/seating system
- Contracture management
- Anti-lock brakes, anti-tippers
- Integrating rest or movement periods out of chairs to avoid "slumping" and fatigue

### Cognitive Impairments: Safety awareness and Communication

- Abilities Care Approach; reducing behaviors
- Heartmath: Heart Rate Variability Training
- Improving ability to express wants/needs
- Use of communication supports

### Environmental Modifications

- Establishing visual contrast
- Rearrangement of furniture & removal of clutter
- Modification of the closet
- Lighting/Temperature
