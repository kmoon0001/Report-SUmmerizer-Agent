## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

- To prevent wound development and preserve highest level of skin integrity
- To expediate or optimize edema control
- To facilitate a ONE Clinical lymphedema approach at facilities for best outcome
- To promote patient and caregiver education and understanding of disease process to be able to self-manage and reduce risk of infection and hospitalization
- To reduce the risk of complications associated with disease processes (infections, wounds, pain, etc.)

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### Limb circumferential measurements with volumetric calculations:

Using a standard tape measure, circumferential measures are taken at pre-determined distances along the length of the limb. Each circumference is used in a geometric formula for a cylinder which enables calculation of the limb volume.

### Pitting Edema Scale:

- 0: No pitting (no indentation remains after pressure)
- 1+: Slight pitting (indentation up to 2 mm deep, rebounds immediately)
- 2+: Moderate pitting (indentation 3-4 mm deep, rebounds in 10-15 seconds)
- 3+: Deep pitting (indentation 5-6 mm deep, rebounds in 15-60 seconds)
- 4+: Very deep pitting (indentation 7-8 mm deep or more, rebounds in over 60 seconds)

### Stemmer's Sign:

A diagnostic test for lymphedema: inability to pinch the skin at the base of the second toe or finger

### Lymphedema Staging

Based on the International Society of Lymphology:

- Stage 0: Latent-subclinical, no visible swelling
- Stage 1: Spontaneously reversible swelling with elevation
- Stage 2: Spontaneously irreversible swelling that does not resolve with elevation, fibrosis begins
- Stage 3: Lymphostatic Elephantiasis-severe swelling, non-pitting, skin changes (e.g., papillomatosis)

### Golematis Staging:

- <2 centimeters - Mild lymphedema
- 2.1 to 4 centimeters - Moderate lymphedema
- > 4.1 centimeters - Severe lymphedema

### Skin and Tissue Assessment

- Texture (soft vs. indurated/fibrotic)
- Presence of scars, radiation fibrosis, or wounds

### Ankle Brachial Index (ABI)

- > 1.30 - Noncompressible. Calcified, likely from long-standing diabetes
- 1.00-1.29 - Normal
- 0.91-0.99 - Borderline. Consider risk and symptoms before compression
- 0.81-0.90 - Mild PAD, possible intermittent claudication. Compression appropriate if asymptomatic
- 0.5-0.80 - Moderate PAD, claudication expected. Compression if validated by vascular referral
- < 0.5 - Significant perfusion change. Compression is not recommended. High risk and vascular assessment needed

### Bioimpedance Spectroscopy (BIS) & Perometry

Performed outside of facility. BIS measures extracellular fluid levels and is useful for early detection and monitoring. Perometry uses infrared light to measure limb volume.

## INTERVENTION/IMPLEMENTATION

### Manual Therapy:

- Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD) which stimulates the movement of fluids in the tissues using gentle massage
- Comprehensive Decongestive Therapy (CDT) which is a combination of MLD, bandaging, skin care and exercises

### Compression:

- Multi-layer compression wraps (keep in mind Rule of LaPlace)
- Velcro garments
- Stockings
- IPC -Intermittent pneumatic compression therapy (pump/garment)

### Positioning:

- Limb elevation- positioning the affected arm or leg above the heart to help drain lymph fluid and reduce swelling

### Exercise:

- Low intensity and repetitive-should be done with compression bandages on the limb
- Encourage gentle muscle pumping, which aids the absorption and transport of the lymphatic fluid out of the limb
- Ankle pumps, ROM, sit to stands, weighted/resistance band strengthening exercises, ambulation

### Skin Care:

- Promotes healthy tissue and prevents skin breakdown and potential infections
- Moisturize the tissue, keep it clean, and attend to small cuts to prevent infections or chronic wounds

### Self-care:

- Self-MLD (Manual Lymphatic Drainage)
- Consistent Compression Garment and/or Bandaging
- Regular Exercise
- Meticulous Skin Care
- Regular Follow-up Visits with Medical Provider and/or Therapist
- Be aware of constrictive clothing and heat to involved limb to decrease risk of recurring lymphedema
- National Lymphedema Network

### Modalities: E-stim:

- Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (NMES)
- Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES)
- Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS)
- Microcurrent Therapy
- Contraindication: previously radiated
