## PURPOSE OF THERAPY

### PT/OT:

- Exercise prescriptions – aerobic conditioning, resistance training, flexibility exercises
- Breathing Exercises to improve peak work capacity
- Balance and Gait training
- Postural management for pain management and respiratory ease – UE positioning with shoulders in adduction, extension and internal rotation will facilitate diaphragmatic breathing
- Positioning for adequate respiration at rest and with activity – lumbar rolls to promote anterior pelvic tilt
- Facilitate conversational speech
- Facilitate sustained phonation
- Improve coordination of breathing with speech and/or swallowing
- Improve Cough Strength for airway protection
- Improve Vocal Quality and/or Loudness Swallow Interventions
- Cognitive interventions strategies related to respiratory function, use of respiratory equipment or management of disease
- Assessment of ADLs and impact respiratory diagnosis has on performance
- Energy conservation and work simplification training
- Psychosocial adaptations
- Relaxation Techniques
- Modalities

## GOAL EXAMPLES

### PT/OT Goals:

- Pt. will complete breath support exercises (name specific exercise) with (% accuracy) to sustain normal volume during conversational speech.
- Pt. will increase maximum phonation time to greater than 15 seconds to improve breath support for speech at a conversational level.
- Pt. will demonstrate increased O2 saturation from 93% to greater than 95% following completion of (name specific) breathing strategy.
- Pt. will use pacing strategies during conversational speech with caregivers with (%) accuracy.
- Patient will utilize necessary adaptive equipment to safely complete UB/LB dressing tasks with minimal assistance while maintaining oxygen saturation levels > 90% with minimal verbal cues for breathing techniques.
- Patient will independently perform breathing technique to maintain oxygen saturation levels above 90% and to minimize SOB for improved carryover during ADL, IADL, and functional mobility
- Patient will demonstrate stress reduction strategies with minimal cues, to reduce anxiety and decrease respiratory rate with 100% carryover.
- Patient will reduce RPE to less than 12/20 post seated showering task with occasional verbal cues for breathing techniques and energy conservation/work simplification.
- Patient will complete self care tasks within 30 minutes, with less than 2 rest breaks, while maintaining oxygen saturation levels >90% on room air.
- Patient will have a decreased RPE (10-11) with household ambulation distances with Supervision to allow mobility without evidence of respiratory distress.
- Patient's six-minute walk test will improve to _______ score to allow for improved activity tolerance with facility mobility tasks.

### SLP Goals:

- Patient will improve speech intelligibility for phrases to 90% to improve communication of needs
- Patient will demonstrate functional voice quality as measured by VHI during short conversations to improve ease of communication
- Patient will use RMST to facilitate improved vocal loudness during communication of needs via improved respiratory strength as measured by RMST device and MPT
- Patient will state 3 scenarios where they have increased oxygen needs to improve resp. function during daily tasks. (cog)
- Patient will improve cough strength via RMST to decrease risk of airway compromise during PO intake of thin liquids
- Will demonstrate adequate coordination of breathing and swallowing on 8/10 therapeutic PO intake trials in order to improve safety and reduce risk of respiratory compromise.
- Patient will report decreased modified Borg scale score(< 3) during PO intake in order to improve endurance for safe and efficient swallow function.
- Patient will adequately manage secretions during therapy session, as evidenced by no need for suction during 30 min session to improve swallow function for secretion management.

## TOOLS & ASSESSMENTS

### PT/OT Tools:

- ACP Plus Respiratory Assessment (ARA) – measure forced inspiratory volume, forced expiratory volume, forced vital capacity
- Provide obstructive and restrictive interventions accordingly – forced expiration, rhythmical breathing, cough
- Omni-Flow
- Incentive Spirometry
- Diaphragmatic and pursed lip breathing Manual chest wall mobilization
- Peak Flow Meter
- Assessment of Posture
- Modified BORG Dyspnea Scale
- MOCA or SLUMS to determine baseline cognitive functioning
- Vital Signs – pulse oximetry, respiratory rate, blood pressure, heart rate
- 6 minute walk test
- 2 min step test
- BERG
- Modified Fatigue Impact Scale
- COPD Self Efficacy Scale
- Chair Rise Test
- Canadian Occupational Performance Measure (COPM)
- E stim for improving muscle performance and exercise tolerance
- Vital Signs

### Motor Speech Assessment

- Cranial Nerve
- Newcastle Motor Speech
- Tifosky word list
- Rainbow or Grandfather Passage
- Colorado Motor Speech Framework

### Voice

- GRBAS
- VHI
- CAPE-V
- S/Z ratio
- MIP/MEP if assessing for RMST or use device to obtain starting point

### Respiratory related approaches for Speech/Voice

- RMST
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Respiratory-speech coordination
- LSVT LOUD or SPEAK OUT!
- Positioning
- Voice Relaxation

### Cognition

- Neuro-Qol PROMIS Cognitive Function
- Modified Fatigue Impact Scale
- Multifactorial Memory Questionnaire
- Communicative Participation Item Bank
- La Trobe Communication Questionnaire
- Iowa Trail Making Test
- Stroop Test
- Community Integration Measure
- Hopkins Verbal Learning
- Royal Prince Alfred Prospective Memory Test
- Baseline general cognitive function

### Respiratory Related approaches for Cognition

- Meta-cognitive strategy to manage respiratory needs
- Memory strategies and external memory aids to recall respiratory needs
- Sequencing for use of respiratory equipment
- Problem solving related to respiratory needs

### Swallowing

- MASA
- Eat-10
- PEFr-peak expiratory flow rate for cough
- 3 ounce Water test
- Test of Masticating and Swallowing Solids(TOMASS)
- Cranial Nerve Exam
- FEEs if needed
- MEP/MIP for Respiratory baseline if using RMST or use device for baseline

### Respiratory related approaches for dysphagia

- RMST-For Swallow and respiratory muscle strengths
- VCST-voluntary cough skill training Breathing-Swallow Coordination
- Strategies such as Supra-glottic swallow
