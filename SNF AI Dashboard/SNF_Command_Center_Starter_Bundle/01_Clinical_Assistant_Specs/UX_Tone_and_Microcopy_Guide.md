# UX Tone And Microcopy Guide

## Purpose

Define plain-language standards for assistant prompts, labels, and user-facing messages.

## Tone

- calm
- direct
- supportive
- non-blaming
- time-aware
- safety-first

## Style Rules

- Prefer 6th to 8th grade reading level where clinically safe.
- Use action-first phrasing.
- Keep labels short but specific.
- Avoid idioms and slang.
- Avoid unexplained abbreviations unless they are standard for the audience.

## Message Patterns

- Welcome: explain what the system can do in one sentence, then offer choices.
- Clarification: ask one focused question at a time.
- Error: explain what failed, what was preserved, and what the user can do next.
- Escalation: state why a human review is required and who should act.
- Empty state: explain why there is no result and what input is missing.
