# CMS SNF Services MLN Documentation Requirements

**Source:** CMS Medicare Learning Network (MLN) Matters — Skilled Nursing Facility Services Documentation Requirements; CMS MLN SE1423, SE1436, and SNF-specific MLN Matters articles
**Scope:** Documentation checklist requirements for SNF providers billing Medicare Part A and Part B services

---

## Overview — Why MLN Documentation Standards Matter

CMS publishes Medicare Learning Network (MLN) Matters articles to clarify documentation expectations for providers. Non-compliance with MLN-documented standards is a leading cause of SNF Medicare denials and recoupment. Surveyors and recovery auditors use these standards as a benchmark.

---

## Core Documentation Requirements Per Visit / Encounter

For every billable SNF therapy or skilled nursing visit, documentation must include:

### Minimum Required Elements
| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Date of service | Must be the actual date of the treatment |
| Service provider name and credentials | Therapist or nurse who rendered the service |
| Service duration | Minutes of direct service time |
| Type of service | Specific procedures or modalities delivered |
| Clinical rationale | Why this service is skilled and medically necessary |
| Patient response | Objective data reflecting patient's response |
| Goal progress | Progress toward documented functional goals |
| Signature | Treating clinician's signature with date |

---

## Face-to-Face Encounter Requirements

**Physician/NPP Face-to-Face Certification (Part A):**
- Required at SNF admission to certify that a qualifying hospital stay occurred and that the patient requires skilled care
- Re-certification required every 30 days (at or before Day 14, Day 30, Day 60, Day 90, etc.)
- Must include the patient's medical condition, current skilled-care needs, estimated recovery period, and plan of care

**Face-to-face documentation must contain:**
- Date of encounter
- Provider name and credentials
- Patient's current medical status
- Clinical basis for skilled-care need
- Signed and dated certification statement

## Supporting Documentation Requirements

In addition to point-of-care notes, the following supporting documentation is required:

### Intake and Evaluation
- **Initial evaluation** by PT, OT, or SLP at start of care, including:
  - Objective baseline measurements
  - Standardized assessment findings (e.g., FIM, Barthel, Berg Balance, MMSE, FOIS)
  - Functional deficits linked to skilled care needs
  - Proposed plan of care with goals and projected frequency/duration

### Ongoing Treatment Notes
- Dated, signed entries for each session
- Objective data: ROM, strength scores, distance ambulated, assistance level, cue level
- Progress or regression compared to baseline
- Safety concerns and clinical decisions made during the session
- Changes to treatment approach and rationale

### Discharge Summary
- Summary of care provided during the episode
- Functional status at discharge vs. admission baseline
- Achievement of goals: met / partially met / not met with clinical reason
- Home program or carryover plan
- Communication to receiving provider/setting

## Common Documentation Deficiencies (MLN-Identified)

Per CMS and MLN guidance, the following deficiencies trigger the highest rates of denial:

1. **Missing or illegible signatures** on treatment notes
2. **Notes that do not reflect skilled care** — generic entries such as "patient tolerated treatment well" without clinical detail
3. **Goals that are not measurable** or are not updated once target dates pass
4. **No documented link** between the clinical condition and the specific skilled intervention
5. **Missing physician certification** or certification that is not dated or signed correctly
6. **Billing minutes that do not match documentation** — the documented minutes must support the billed code
7. **Provider signature without credentials** — always include licensure level (e.g., PT, COTA, RN)

## Part B Therapy Documentation Requirements

For Medicare Part B outpatient therapy in the SNF:
- The same documentation elements apply as Part A, but certification rules differ
- The therapist must document the functional limitation and clinical rationale at the appropriate level of functional severity using G-codes (where still applicable) and now using PT/OT/SLP required elements under MIPS/QPP
- Timed and untimed service codes must be supported by the documented minutes and service type
- Group therapy must be documented separately from individual therapy with appropriate group code

---

## Documentation Retention Requirements

- SNF medical records must be retained for **a minimum of 6 years** from the date of service or until the minor patient reaches the age of 21 (whichever is longer), per federal requirements
- State law may impose longer retention obligations — always follow the stricter requirement
- Records must be available for audit within a reasonable timeframe (typically 30 days of a post-payment review request)
