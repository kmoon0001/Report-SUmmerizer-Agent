# AOTA Documentation Standards for Occupational Therapy Services in SNF Settings

**Source:** American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA) — Guidelines for Documentation of Occupational Therapy (AOTA, 2018, updated); AOTA Practice Standards; Occupational Therapy Practice Framework: Domain and Process, 4th Edition (OTPF-4)
**Scope:** OT documentation requirements in skilled nursing facility and post-acute care settings with Medicare compliance

---

## Overview — AOTA Documentation Framework

AOTA documentation standards require OT services to be clearly defined in terms of the client's **occupational roles**, **activities of daily living (ADLs)**, **instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs)**, and **participation restrictions** caused by the medical condition. In SNF settings, these standards must align with CMS Medicare documentation requirements.

---

## OT Evaluation Documentation Requirements

### Mandatory Elements of the OT Evaluation:
- **Referral and reason for OT services** — the specific occupational performance issue or concern
- **Occupational profile** — patient's prior roles, daily routines, values, and priorities
- **Medical history and diagnosis** relevant to the OT evaluation
- **Occupational performance analysis** — how the medical condition is limiting ADL and IADL performance
- **Standardized assessment results** with raw scores, standard scores, and norms:
  - Cognitive: MMSE, MOCA, KELS, LOTCA, Allen Cognitive Levels
  - ADL/Functional: FIM (motor and cognitive subscales), Barthel Index, AMPS, Kohlman
  - UE function: DASH, Box and Block, Purdue Pegboard, grip strength
  - Perceptual: MVPT, BIT (Behavioral Inattention Test)
- **Client and caregiver goals** in the patient's own words
- **OT diagnosis / problem list** with occupational barriers clearly described
- **Plan of care** with measurable long-term and short-term goals, frequency, and duration
- **Prognosis statement** based on clinical presentation and medical context
- **Clinician signature, credentials, and date**

## Functional ADL Documentation

AOTA and CMS require that OT documentation explicitly link the patient's clinical deficits to occupational limitations:

**Required ADL documentation format:**
- Baseline level of assistance: Independent / Modified Independent / Supervision / Min Assist / Mod Assist / Max Assist / Total Assist / Dependent
- Specific functional task evaluated (e.g., "Upper body dressing," "toileting," "dynamic sitting balance during grooming")
- Objective data supporting the assistance level (e.g., "Patient required moderate assistance of 1 to don shirt secondary to left UE weakness with grip strength 2/5 MMT")
- Environmental and contextual factors affecting performance

---

## Treatment Note Requirements

Each OT treatment session must document:
- Date, duration, and service type (individual, group, co-treatment, concurrent)
- Specific ADLs, IADLs, or OT goals addressed
- Treatment techniques and adaptive strategies used (e.g., compensatory strategy training, energy conservation, adaptive equipment trial)
- Objective performance data with comparison to baseline and previous session
- Progress toward short-term and long-term goals
- Patient and caregiver education provided and response to instruction
- Clinical decisions and plan modifications
- Safety observations and fall risk considerations
- Signature, credentials, and date

## Medicare Coverage for OT in SNF

OT services are covered under Medicare Part A and Part B when:
1. Services are **medically necessary** for the patient's diagnosis and functional decline
2. The services **require the skills of a qualified OT or COTA under OT supervision**
3. Services are **ordered by a physician or NPP** under an approved plan of care
4. Documentation demonstrates **skilled OT intervention** — not just assistance with ADLs, but clinical analysis and adaptation requiring professional judgment

**Covered OT conditions in SNF:** Functional decline after orthopedic surgery, CVA/TBI-related ADL limitations, progressive neurological decline, cognitive impairment affecting ADL safety, upper extremity dysfunction, and dysphagia when within OT scope

## PDPM Considerations for OT

Under PDPM, OT documentation directly supports:
- **Section GG (Functional Abilities and Goals)** on the MDS — OT should contribute to the baseline and discharge GG scores
- OT classification into the OT-component payment group
- OT minutes reported in therapy volume tracking
- Clinical justification for any change in OT classification during the stay

---

## Discharge Documentation for OT

OT discharge summary must include:
- Summary of OT services provided during the episode
- Functional status at discharge vs. admission (objective comparison using standardized data)
- Goal outcomes: met / partially met / not met with clinical rationale
- Adaptive equipment recommended and trained during the stay
- Home modifications recommended
- Caregiver training summary — who was trained, what was covered, demonstrated competence
- Follow-up OT recommendations for next care setting
- Communication to the receiving provider
