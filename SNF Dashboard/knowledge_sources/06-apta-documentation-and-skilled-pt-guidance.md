# APTA Documentation Standards for Physical Therapy in SNF Settings

**Source:** American Physical Therapy Association (APTA) — Guidelines for Physical Therapy Documentation of Patient/Client Management (APTA, 2014, revised); APTA Guide to Physical Therapist Practice 3.0; APTA PDPM educational resources
**Scope:** PT documentation requirements in skilled nursing facility and post-acute care settings with Medicare compliance and PDPM alignment

---

## Overview — APTA Documentation Principles

APTA documentation standards require PT services to be clinically specific, legally defensible, and clearly linked to the patient's functional goals. In SNF settings, PT documentation must simultaneously satisfy APTA professional standards and CMS Medicare coverage requirements.

---

## PT Evaluation Documentation Requirements

### Required Elements of the PT Initial Evaluation:

**Patient/Client History:**
- Chief complaint and reason for referral
- Prior level of function (PLOF) before the precipitating event
- Relevant surgical, medical, and medication history
- Social history: living situation, support system, prior activity level, occupation or volunteer roles
- Patient goals in the patient's own words

**Systems Review:**
- Cardiovascular/pulmonary screen
- Integumentary screen (especially relevant for bed mobility and wound risk)
- Musculoskeletal screen: gross ROM and strength
- Neuromuscular screen: coordination, balance, sensation
- Communication and behavioral/cognitive screen

**Tests and Measures (objective data with standardized assessments):**
- Muscle strength: MMT scale (0-5) for relevant muscle groups
- Range of motion: goniometric measurements for restricted joints
- Balance: Berg Balance Scale, TUG (Timed Up and Go), POMA, SPPB, MCTSIB
- Gait: 6-Minute Walk Test, 10-Meter Walk Test, gait speed (m/s), assistive device used
- Functional mobility: Transfer ability (bed to sit, sit to stand, w/c to bed), ambulation distance and quality
- Pain: Numeric Rating Scale or FACES scale
- Functional Independence: FIM motor subscale, Barthel Index

**Assessment:**
- PT diagnosis using appropriate diagnostic classification
- Functional limitations and participation restrictions clearly described
- Prognosis: anticipated functional outcome, timeline, and factors affecting prognosis

**Plan:**
- Long-term goals with measurable outcomes and target dates
- Short-term goals progressing toward long-term goals
- Intervention plan: specific modalities, therapeutic exercises, and functional training
- Frequency and duration of treatment
- Physician/NPP certification obtained

## Treatment Note Documentation

Each PT treatment session must document:
- Date, duration (minutes), and service category (individual, group, concurrent)
- Specific interventions delivered (with the clinical rationale if different from the plan)
- Objective patient performance data:
  - Repetitions and sets, resistance levels, assistance level, assistive device used
  - Distance ambulated, speed, and number of rest breaks
  - Balance performance on standardized or functional tasks
  - Pain level before and after treatment
- Progress toward short-term goals with numeric comparison to prior documented session
- Patient response and tolerance to treatment
- Clinical decisions made during the session (e.g., decreased resistance due to pain, added hip hinge training due to fall pattern)
- Safety incidents or near-miss events with full description
- Clinician signature, credential, and date

## Medicare Coverage for PT in SNF

PT services are covered under Medicare Part A and Part B when:
1. The services are **medically necessary** for the patient's diagnosis and functional status
2. The services **require the skills of a licensed PT or qualified PTA under PT supervision**
3. Services are **ordered by a physician or NPP** and documented on an approved plan of care
4. Documentation supports **skilled PT intervention** — clinical complexity, decision-making, and adaptation that cannot be safely performed by non-licensed staff

**Covered PT conditions in SNF:** Post-surgical orthopedic rehabilitation (THR, TKR, ORIF), neurological rehabilitation (CVA, TBI, Parkinson's disease), balance and fall risk reduction, deconditioning, cardiopulmonary reconditioning, and wound care requiring PT assessment

## PDPM PT Component Documentation

Under PDPM, PT documentation directly supports:
- **PT payment group classification** based on primary diagnosis, prior functional status (Section GG), and comorbidities
- **Section GG baseline and discharge scoring** — PT contributes objective GG ratings for mobility items
- PT minutes documentation for volume tracking (though minutes are not the primary PDPM driver)
- Clinical documentation of **comorbidities** that affect PT complexity and classify to higher payment groups (certain neurological, respiratory, or cardiovascular conditions)

**Section GG PT focus areas:**
- Transfer ability (sit-to-stand, toilet transfer, car transfer)
- Ambulation on level surfaces
- Ambulation on stairs (if applicable to the discharge environment)
- Wheelchair mobility (if relevant)

## Fall Risk and Prevention Documentation

Falls are a primary PT focus in SNF. Documentation should include:
- Fall risk screen result (Morse Fall Scale, TUG, POMA score, or validated tool used by the facility)
- Contributing factors identified (e.g., polypharmacy, orthostatic hypotension, lower extremity weakness, balance deficit, vision impairment, footwear)
- Specific PT interventions to address identified fall risk factors
- Documentation of any falls that occurred during the stay, including the PT response and care-plan update
- Staff and caregiver education provided regarding fall prevention strategies

---

## Discharge Documentation for PT

PT discharge summary must include:
- Summary of PT interventions during the episode
- Functional status at discharge vs. admission using the same objective measures
- Goal outcomes: met / partially met / not met with clinical reason
- Assistive device in use at discharge (with patient competency noted)
- Home program provided: exercises, activity restrictions, progression criteria
- Recommended follow-up PT (outpatient, home health) with clinical rationale
- Communication to the receiving provider or next care setting
