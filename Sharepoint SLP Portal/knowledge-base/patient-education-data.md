# Patient Education

- Simple steps to make your home safer and reduce your risk of falling.
- Falls are a leading cause of injury in older adults. Many falls can be prevented by making simple changes to your home and daily habits.
- Check your home for these common tripping hazards:
- Remove throw rugs or secure them with non-slip tape
- Keep electrical cords and phone wires out of walkways
- Pick up clutter from floors and stairs
- Keep frequently used items at waist height (not on high shelves)
- Wear shoes with good grip (avoid slippers and socks alone)
- Good lighting helps you see obstacles and stay steady:
- Install bright lights in hallways and bathrooms
- Use nightlights in bedrooms and bathrooms
- Keep light switches easily accessible
- Install grab bars near toilet and in shower/tub
- Use a shower chair or bench
- Consider a raised toilet seat
- Make sure all stairs are safe and well-maintained:
- Install handrails on both sides of stairs
- Make sure stairs are well-lit
- Mark the edge of steps with bright tape
- Keep stairs clear of clutter
- Consider a stair lift if stairs are difficult

- Tips for managing back pain and staying active safely.
- Most back pain improves with time and activity. Staying active is usually better than bed rest. Your physical therapist will help you find safe ways to move.
- Good posture reduces strain on your back muscles:
- Sit with your shoulders relaxed and back against the chair
- Keep your feet flat on the floor
- Avoid slouching or leaning forward
- When standing, keep your shoulders back and core engaged
- Change positions frequently throughout the day
- Always Use these steps when lifting items:
- Bend at your knees, not your back
- Keep the object close to your body
- Tighten your core muscles before lifting
- Avoid twisting while holding something heavy
- Ask for help with heavy items
- Try these methods to manage your back pain comfortably:
- Apply ice for the first 48 hours after injury
- Switch to heat after 48 hours if helpful
- Try gentle stretching and movement
- Use a supportive pillow when sleeping
- Contact your doctor if you have numbness, tingling, weakness, or loss of bladder/bowel control.
- What to expect after knee surgery and how to support your recovery.
- Recovery from knee surgery takes time. Most people see significant improvement in 6-12 weeks, but full recovery can take 6-12 months. Follow your physical therapist
- Focus on managing pain and swelling during early recovery:
- Keep your leg elevated when sitting or lying down
- Use crutches or walker as directed
- Attend all physical therapy appointments
- Gradually increase mobility as your knee heals:
- Work on bending and straightening your knee
- Start walking with less support
- Continue ice and elevation as needed
- Return to normal activities with improved strength:
- Work on balance and stability
- Contact your surgeon if you experience increased swelling, warmth, redness, fever, or severe pain not relieved by medication.
- Tips for safe movement and daily activities after stroke.
- After a stroke, you may have weakness on one side of your body. Your physical therapist will teach you safe ways to move and prevent falls.
- Follow these steps to safely move from bed to sitting:
- Roll onto your stronger side
- Push yourself up with your stronger arm
- Swing your legs over the edge of the bed
- Sit for a moment before standing
- Use a walker or cane if needed
- Use these tips to regain your walking independence:
- Use a cane or walker as recommended
- Have someone nearby when first learning to walk
- Stay active and positive during your home recovery:
- Practice exercises your therapist taught you daily
- Use your weaker arm and leg as much as possible
- Take breaks to avoid fatigue
- Ask for help when needed
- Keep a positive attitude - recovery takes time
- Call 911 if you experience sudden weakness, numbness, difficulty speaking, vision changes, or severe headache.
- Safe exercise tips for managing arthritis pain and maintaining mobility.
- Regular exercise can reduce arthritis pain, improve flexibility, strengthen muscles, and help you maintain a healthy weight. Start slowly and gradually increase activity.
- Schedule your exercise for maximum benefit and minimum pain:
- Exercise when pain is lowest (usually mid-morning)
- Warm up with gentle movement for 5-10 minutes
- Exercise on most days of the week
