# Skilled Necessity And Compliance Guide

## Purpose
Reference for defensible SNF therapy documentation. Use this source to explain what makes therapy skilled, what creates denial risk, and how to strengthen medical necessity language.

## Skilled Elements To Show
- Therapist analysis and clinical decision making
- Need for skilled instruction, cueing, progression, modification, or monitoring
- Measurable functional performance
- Response to treatment
- Safety risk, barriers, or precautions when relevant
- Rationale for continued treatment or discharge planning

## Common Weak Phrases
- Tolerated treatment well
- Worked on balance
- Completed exercises
- Patient participated

## Stronger Replacements
- Required CGA and verbal cues for safe turn negotiation with RW due to impaired dynamic balance.
- Needed skilled instruction to apply compensatory swallowing strategies consistently during PO trials.
- Demonstrated improved toileting task completion with reduced physical assist but continued sequencing deficits requiring OT intervention.

## Common Denial Risks
- No skilled rationale
- No measurable function
- No response to treatment
- Unsupported progress claims
- Boilerplate wording repeated across visits
