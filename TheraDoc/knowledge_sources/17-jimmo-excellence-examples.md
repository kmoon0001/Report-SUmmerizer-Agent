# Jimmo Excellence Examples

## Purpose
Positive-reference examples for documentation that supports skilled maintenance therapy under Jimmo principles. Use these patterns to reinforce defensible clinical reasoning, not to copy patient facts.

## Example Pattern A: Skilled Maintenance PT
- Prior status and current objective measures are explicit.
- Skilled intervention and therapist-specific decision making are documented.
- Medical necessity ties treatment intensity to safety and function.
- Next-session plan includes measurable targets and risk mitigation.

## Example Pattern B: Skilled Maintenance OT
- Functional ADL barriers are specific and quantifiable.
- Therapist cueing strategy and adaptation rationale are clear.
- Documentation explains why skilled OT services remain required.
- Progress limits and barriers are linked to concrete follow-up actions.

## Example Pattern C: Skilled Maintenance SLP
- Swallow or cognitive-communication targets are measurable.
- Response to skilled cues and task progression are explicit.
- Narrative explains skilled need despite chronic or maintenance status.
- Ongoing plan aligns with safety risk and interdisciplinary handoff needs.

## Jimmo Alignment Checklist
1. Document what skilled therapy is being delivered now.
2. Document why the skills of a therapist are required now.
3. Document measurable response and remaining barriers.
4. Document why maintenance treatment remains clinically necessary.
